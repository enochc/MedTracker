# Screenshot Instructions

Google Play requires at least **2 screenshots** for your app listing.

## How to Take Screenshots

### Option 1: Using Android Studio Emulator
1. Open Android Studio
2. Launch your app in an emulator (Pixel 5 or similar recommended)
3. Use the camera icon in the emulator toolbar to capture screenshots
4. Screenshots are saved to your desktop

### Option 2: Using a Physical Device
1. Install the app on your Android device
2. Take screenshots using your device's screenshot function:
   - Most devices: **Power + Volume Down** simultaneously
3. Transfer screenshots to your computer via USB or cloud storage

## Recommended Screenshots

Take these screenshots to showcase your app:

### Screenshot 1: Home Screen with Medication List
- Show the main screen with 2-3 medications listed
- Display the "Add Medication" button
- Should demonstrate the clean, simple interface

### Screenshot 2: Widget on Home Screen
- Place the widget on your home screen
- Show medication name and last taken time
- Demonstrates the key feature of quick access

### Screenshot 3: Medication Confirmation Dialog (Optional but Recommended)
- Show the "Take Medication?" confirmation screen
- Display the pre-filled amount field
- Shows the confirm and cancel buttons
- Highlights the History link

### Screenshot 4: History Screen (Optional)
- Display the medication history log
- Shows timestamps and amounts taken
- Demonstrates tracking capability

## Screenshot Specifications

- **Format**: PNG or JPEG
- **Minimum Dimensions**: 320px on shortest side
- **Maximum Dimensions**: 3840px on longest side
- **Aspect Ratio**: Between 16:9 and 9:16
- **Recommended**: 1080x1920 (portrait) or 1920x1080 (landscape)

## Tips for Great Screenshots

1. **Use Light Theme**: Ensure your device is in light mode for better visibility
2. **Hide Status Bar Info**: Consider hiding personal info (time, battery, notifications)
3. **Add Sample Data**: Use realistic medication names and times
4. **Keep it Clean**: No personal information or inappropriate content
5. **Frame Devices** (Optional): Tools like Mockuphone.com can add device frames

## After Taking Screenshots

1. Save them with descriptive names:
   - `screenshot-1-home.png`
   - `screenshot-2-widget.png`
   - `screenshot-3-confirm.png`
   - `screenshot-4-history.png`

2. Upload them to Google Play Console in the order you want them displayed

3. You can add captions to each screenshot in the Play Console to explain features
